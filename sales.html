The Gap in Every Other Tool

What you're working without
every time you analyse a deal.

Every other property calculator on the market shows you yield and cash flow. That's the starting point, not the analysis. Serious investors need the full picture.

01
You're looking at yield. You should be looking at IRR.

Gross yield is what estate agents quote. IRR is what institutional investors actually decide on. A 7% gross yield deal might have a 9% IRR — below your hurdle rate — once you factor in transaction costs, tax drag, vacancy, capital growth, and exit proceeds. Without IRR you're comparing apples to guesses.

02
You're ignoring the CGT you'll pay on the way out.

Every property analysis that doesn't model exit is incomplete. What structure do you hold it in? What does CGT actually cost at sale — after your agent's commission and bond settlement? What do you actually walk away with? These questions change the deal. Most investors answer them after they've already committed.

03
You're not stress-testing anything.

What happens to your cash flow if rates go up 200 basis points? What if vacancy is 3% higher than you assumed? What if both happen simultaneously? A deal that looks fine on base assumptions can become a serious cash drain under realistic stress. If you can't see the matrix, you don't know your exposure.
